Upcoming events:

In honor of Joan Feynman: Women in astrophysics

Mon, January 12

Practicum Library Conference Hall & Online

At this event, which celebrates the library's namesake, our guest lecturer will take us on a journey exploring some of the most prominent female researchers in the world of space science. It's easy to buy into the misconception that this field was mostly dominated by men, but our lecture aims to change that notion and prove that this hasn't been the case since at least the 18th century.

Reader picks

From the Library Collection of Kory Russell

Princess: True Story behind the Veil in Saudi Arabia

This extraordinary biography is based on a secret friendship between an American author and a Saudi Princess who is granted a venue to tell her story through her hierachical eyes in the treatment of women in the Kingdom of Saudi Arabia. This is a great for the readers in Human Rights.

Jean Sasson

Murder on the Titanic

This fictional novel based on a true tragedy is packed with suspense, thrilling turn-arounds, and a true mystery of a man born into wealth, honest to a fault and humble for his class. A must read for "mystery based on historical events" lovers. It will leave you infatuated for the sequal: "Voice from the Titanic."

Jim Walker

The Shepherd of the Hills

This delightful, and down-to-earth book is a vision into The Ozark Mountains region of Southwest Missouri of what is now near the area of Branson, Missouri of the 1880's. The simplicity of life of the time and alternative hardships endured of that era leave you with a new perspective of life today and with all the changes of time, and their stark differences.

Harold Bell Wright

Gifted Hands: The Ben Carson Story

This auto-biography is based on the prominent neuro-surgeon at Johns-Hopkins where the once poor and impoverished young black child loved to read and blossoms into a heroic story that literally uses the old cliche: "From rags to riches" and the struggle through it all.

Ben Carson, M.D., Cecil Murphey
